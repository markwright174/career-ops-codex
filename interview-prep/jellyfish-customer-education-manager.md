## Likely Questions

### Technical / Functional
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you build a 12-18 month customer education roadmap for a product with evolving AI capabilities?
  Best angle: anchor on TSTC program leadership, then explain how you would prioritize by audience, adoption risk, release cadence, and business impact.
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you decide what belongs in Academy courses vs webinars vs in-app guidance vs CSM-led assets?
  Best angle: use your self-paced certification and TSU support-resource work to show channel-aware design.
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you measure whether customer education is actually improving adoption?
  Best angle: be honest that your current CV shows partial evidence on metrics; talk through completion, engagement, support deflection, feature adoption, and learner feedback as a framework.
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you design for technical and analytics-heavy topics without overwhelming learners?
  Best angle: use Codio migration and complex-platform translation examples.

### Role-Specific
- `[inferred from JD]` You have instructional design depth, but where have you directly owned customer education?
  Why they ask: the JD explicitly asks for program ownership in customer education or similar B2B SaaS work.
  Best angle: own the gap, then bridge to self-serve resources, scalable learning paths, support assets, and adoption-oriented design across LMS environments.
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you operate as a team of one?
  Why they ask: the JD says `team of one` and expects both strategy and execution.
  Best angle: point to Alight leadership plus hands-on building across TSU and TSTC.
- `[inferred from JD]` How would you partner with Product, CS, Support, and PMM?
  Why they ask: the JD emphasizes cross-functional ownership.
  Best angle: frame your faculty/stakeholder model as analogous to cross-functional product work.

### Background Red Flags
- `[inferred from JD]` Most of your background is in education. Why move into customer education in SaaS?
  Recommended framing: customer education is not a departure from your work; it is a different commercial context for the same core strength of helping people adopt complex systems.
- `[inferred from JD]` Do you have enough direct product and analytics depth for an engineering-focused platform?
  Recommended framing: you are not claiming engineering-domain expertise; you are claiming technical-learning leadership, fast learning, and a track record of translating complexity into usable learning.
- `[inferred from JD]` Can you be effective without a larger production team behind you?
  Recommended framing: yes, and use the combination of strategy + execution across TSU, UTeach, and Alight.
